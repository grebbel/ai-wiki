# One Health policy communities' perspectives on zoonoses risk factors and management: A multiple qualitative methods approach

## TL;DR

The study maps how policy communities conceptualize zoonoses risk in Peru and shows that shared One Health intent still meets recurring coordination, governance, and power-structure constraints.

## Key findings

- Human, animal, and environmental policy actors identify overlapping zoonoses drivers but weight livestock-related factors more strongly.
- System dynamics synthesis reveals interdependent risk structures that single-sector interventions cannot address well.
- Participants report implementation barriers linked to coordination fragility, funding constraints, and institutional power asymmetries.

## Implementation implications

- One Health governance design should include explicit mechanisms for cross-sector decision rights, not only cross-sector meetings.
- Program design for low-resource rural settings must adapt to local socioeconomic context rather than transfer generic templates.
