# IPCC AR6 Synthesis Report: Climate Change 2023

## TL;DR

The IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6) Synthesis Report provides the most comprehensive and authoritative assessment of current climate science, impacts, and adaptation/mitigation strategies across human, animal, and environmental domains—directly relevant to One Health and biosecurity planning under climate uncertainty.

## Key findings

- **Global warming trajectory**: Climate systems are fundamentally altered by emissions; every 0.1°C of additional warming matters
- **Climate impact cascades**: Warming triggers cascading impacts across food systems, water availability, disease vectors, and economic stability
- **Tipping points**: Several climate systems approach irreversible thresholds (ice sheets, ocean circulation, Amazon forest transitions)
- **Health security nexus**: Climate change amplifies pathogen vectors (e.g., Zika, dengue, malaria ranges shifting), agricultural risks (crop failures, livestock stress), and migration/conflict drivers
- **Mitigation urgency**: Rapid deep cuts in greenhouse gas emissions are essential; adaptation alone cannot prevent severe impacts
- **Window closing**: Remaining carbon budget for 1.5°C scenario exhausts within years; 2°C scenarios require action within current decade

## Implementation implications

- **Biosecurity perspective**: Climate change is a force multiplier for zoonotic disease emergence (expanding vector habitats, wildlife range shifts, spillover opportunities)
- **One Health integration**: Health security planning must incorporate climate projections; pandemic preparedness and climate resilience are linked
- **Supply chain vulnerability**: Agricultural and food security risks are compounded by climate impacts; food system diversity is critical
- **Cross-border coordination**: Climate-driven migration and resource scarcity will increase coordination demands on health systems
- **Data needs**: National health systems require climate-impact scenarios to assess future disease burden, preparedness scenarios, and resource needs
