# Biosafety and biosecurity as essential pillars of international health security and cross-cutting elements of biological nonproliferation

## TL;DR

This paper argues that biosafety and biosecurity are the practical hinge connecting public-health preparedness with nonproliferation obligations, especially across the IHR, the BWC, and UNSCR 1540.

## Key findings

- The paper maps concrete overlaps between the IHR (2005), the Biological Weapons Convention, and UNSCR 1540.
- It frames laboratory biosafety and biosecurity as shared operational requirements rather than separate policy domains.
- Georgia is used as a case showing that legislation, training, surveillance, and laboratory oversight have to advance together.
- The article treats pathogen accounting, secure storage, workforce training, and incident response as one integrated capability set.

## Implementation implications

- NBRA-style risk assessment should treat biosafety and biosecurity as cross-cutting capacity layers, not isolated compliance topics.
- Inventory systems, laboratory governance, and preparedness planning can be aligned against the same international obligation set.
- Cross-ministerial coordination is necessary because health, security, and legal controls all shape biological-risk reduction.
