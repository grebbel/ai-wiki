# A HACCP-One Health framework for estimating and managing food safety risk in game meat supply chains: Insights from Zambia

## TL;DR

This study maps food-safety hazards across farmed and wild game-meat supply chains in Zambia and shows that wild game pathways carry substantially higher unmanaged risk.

## Key findings

- Wild game supply chains show higher food-safety risk than farmed game due to weak inspections, field slaughter hygiene gaps, and transport-chain weaknesses.
- Farmed game pathways benefit from stronger controls, but cold-chain breaks and delayed evisceration remain critical points.
- A HACCP-informed One Health framing helps connect veterinary, public-health, and environmental controls into one operational risk model.

## Implementation implications

- Priority controls include standardized inspection routines, stronger cold-chain enforcement, and practical hygiene support at decentralized slaughter points.
- Consumer-facing risk communication and regulatory harmonization are needed alongside technical controls.
