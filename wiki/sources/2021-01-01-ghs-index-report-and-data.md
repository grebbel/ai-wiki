# GHS Index Report and Data

## TL;DR

This source is the Global Health Security Index reporting portal, summarizing the 2021 benchmark across 195 countries and the six-category framework used to compare national preparedness for epidemics and pandemics.

## Key findings

- The portal's headline finding is that countries remain broadly underprepared for future epidemic and pandemic threats despite COVID-era capacity gains.
- The framework groups preparedness into prevention, detection, rapid response, health systems, enabling norms and financing, and risk environment.
- The site foregrounds financing gaps, weak health-system maintenance, and exposure of vulnerable populations as persistent weaknesses.
- The portal is designed for comparative navigation across country scores, methods, and category definitions rather than for source-level case description.
- It is useful as a comparative baseline rather than as a country implementation case.

## Implementation implications

- The GHS Index can serve as an external benchmark alongside the more implementation-focused NBRA source set.
- Its category structure helps translate broad preparedness questions into assessable capability domains.
- Because it is a benchmarking portal, it should be used with source-specific evidence rather than as a substitute for operational detail.
- It is especially useful when the wiki needs a comparative framing for preparedness gaps before moving to concrete country or sector examples.
