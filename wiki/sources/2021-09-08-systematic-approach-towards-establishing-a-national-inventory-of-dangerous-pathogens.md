# Systematic approach towards establishing a National Inventory of Dangerous Pathogens

## TL;DR

This paper proposes a practical four-phase method for countries to build and maintain a national inventory of dangerous pathogens as a foundation for stronger biosafety and biosecurity governance.

## Key findings

- A national inventory can be implemented through four phases: identification, preparation, implementation, and maintenance/evaluation.
- The approach emphasizes cross-ministry coordination, a mandated national focal point, and secure data governance.
- The authors report practical implementation lessons from East-African country support work, including Uganda.
- The inventory is positioned as a foundational control layer for compliance with BWC, UNSCR 1540, and IHR obligations.

## Implementation implications

- The first bottleneck is institutional ownership, not software. Early mandate and role clarity determine throughput.
- Cybersecurity design (access controls, secure storage, backup policy, communication channels) must be treated as part of policy design.
- Inventory maintenance is continuous. The value is sustained only if updates are operationalized as a recurring national process.
