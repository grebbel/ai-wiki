# Horizon scanning of invasive plant pests endangering Zimbabwe's agricultural sector based on insights from neighbouring countries

## TL;DR

This paper uses horizon-scanning and pest-risk methods to prioritize invasive pest threats for Zimbabwe and identifies high-risk entry pathways and species for proactive biosecurity planning.

## Key findings

- The scan identifies 73 high-priority invasive pest threats from neighbouring countries with moderate-to-high establishment likelihood.
- Invertebrate species dominate the near-term threat profile.
- Human-mediated movement pathways (including travellers and luggage) are emphasized as major vectors.

## Implementation implications

- Risk-based border screening and pathway-focused surveillance should prioritize highest-scoring pest-pathway pairs.
- Regular horizon-scanning updates can provide a practical early-warning rhythm for national plant-biosecurity programs.
