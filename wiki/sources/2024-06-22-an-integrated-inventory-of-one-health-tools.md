# An integrated inventory of One Health tools: Mapping and analysis of globally available tools to advance One Health

## TL;DR

The paper inventories 50 globally used One Health tools and identifies major implementation gaps, especially around food safety, AMR, environmental integration, and monitoring-oriented tooling.

- Raw type: journalArticle routed to raw/papers.

## Key findings

- From 132 candidate tools, 50 met inclusion criteria for broad One Health applicability.
- Coverage exists across all OH JPA action tracks, but practical support is thinner for food safety, AMR, and environmental integration.
- Existing tools are stronger for assessment than for implementation, prioritization, and monitoring.

## Implementation implications

- National One Health programs should design toolchains, not single-tool deployments, with explicit links from assessment to implementation and monitoring.
- Environmental and social-inclusion dimensions require deliberate augmentation in current tool portfolios.
