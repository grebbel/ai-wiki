# Establishment of a National Inventory of Dangerous Pathogens in the Republic of Uganda

## TL;DR

This paper describes Uganda's implementation of a centralized national pathogen inventory and shows how such a system can support biosecurity monitoring, emergency planning, and compliance with international expectations.

## What was actually ingested

- Source channel: Zotero acquire from collection NBRA.
- Raw type: journalArticle routed to raw/papers.
- Full-text quality: zotero-extracted from the article text.

## Key findings

- Uganda's inventory was built as a secure national database covering controlled dangerous pathogens across multiple laboratories.
- The implementation was driven by biosecurity needs identified through WHO Joint External Evaluation and GHSA follow-up.
- Inventory data is positioned as a basis for monitoring laboratory biosecurity performance, not just for static recordkeeping.
- The paper presents the inventory as a practical, transferable model for countries that need a first national overview of dangerous pathogens.

## Implementation implications

- National pathogen inventories can act as a foundation layer for broader biological-risk governance.
- An inventory becomes more useful when it is connected to emergency operations, reference-lab designation, and monitoring routines.
- The Uganda case strengthens the wiki's existing pathogen-inventory line by adding a second concrete country implementation example.
