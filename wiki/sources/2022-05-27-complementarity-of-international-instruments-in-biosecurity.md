# Complementarity of International Instruments in the Field of Biosecurity

## TL;DR

The paper argues that full IHR implementation improves biosecurity readiness but is insufficient alone for intentional-threat prevention, which requires additional non-proliferation and export-control measures.

## Key findings

- IHR, BWC, and UNSCR 1540 have significant overlap but non-identical obligations.
- Practical compliance requires combining public-health preparedness with explicit non-proliferation controls.
- Non-binding guidance instruments play an implementation-bridging role between legal obligations and operational practice.

## Implementation implications

- National biosecurity architecture should be cross-mapped against multiple treaty regimes, not managed through single-instrument compliance.
- Policy teams need joined-up legal-public-health workflows to reduce blind spots in deliberate-threat readiness.
