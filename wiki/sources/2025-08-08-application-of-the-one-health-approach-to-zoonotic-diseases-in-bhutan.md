# Application of the One Health approach to zoonotic diseases in Bhutan: A qualitative assessment of gaps and opportunities from key stakeholders' perspectives

## TL;DR

This qualitative assessment shows that Bhutan's One Health strategy has a solid policy base but execution is constrained by governance, financing, staffing, and cross-sector information-fragmentation gaps.

## Key findings

- Stakeholders report recurring implementation bottlenecks in funding, staffing, communication systems, and strategic-plan maintenance.
- A weak or under-resourced secretariat function degrades cross-sector coordination despite policy intent.
- Capacity-building, strategy refresh, and sustained financing mechanisms are central leverage points.

## Implementation implications

- One Health plans need periodic strategy refresh cycles tied to operational budgets and accountability mechanisms.
- Communication and data-sharing architecture should be treated as core infrastructure, not adjunct tooling.
